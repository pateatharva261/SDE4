## 1. Learning Objectives

After this lesson you will be able to:

- Design the platform's **observability** (Part 16): metrics, logs, traces — correlated — across all contexts.
- Instrument for the **golden signals + SLO burn-rate alerting** (14.3/14.1) defined in 20.2.
- Trace **distributed flows** (a trade saga — 20.6) end-to-end (16.4) to localize latency + failures.
- Run a **production-readiness review** (PRR): the checklist that gates launch (SLOs, alerting, runbooks, DR-tested, security, capacity).
- Connect observability to **incident response + postmortems** (14.5) and **progressive delivery** (14.7).

---

## 2. Motivation

You **cannot operate what you can't see** — and a financial platform must **detect + diagnose problems fast** (tight MTTR — 14.5), **prove** it meets SLOs (20.2), and **audit** what happened. Observability (Part 16) is the operational nervous system across the microservices platform, and the **production-readiness review** is the discipline that ensures a system is actually **safe to run**, not just built. This lesson makes the platform **operable**.

## 3. The design (framework — 1.3.1)

### 3.1 Three pillars, correlated (16.1)

`[BP]`
- **Metrics** (16.2) — **detect**: golden signals (latency/traffic/errors/saturation — 14.3) + SLO metrics per context (20.2), in a TSDB (the platform's own monitoring — 19.2.6/16.6), with **bounded cardinality** (16.2).
- **Logs** (16.3) — **diagnose**: **structured**, correlation-ID-stamped, secrets/PII-**redacted** (15.8); operational logs distinct from the **immutable audit log** (20.7 — different purpose/retention).
- **Traces** (16.4) — **localize**: distributed traces across service hops (incl. async — Part 9), context-propagated (W3C).
- **Correlated** via **trace IDs** (16.1): jump metrics → traces → logs for a single request. The workflow: **detect (metrics) → localize (traces) → diagnose (logs)**.
- `[BP]` **Three pillars, correlated by trace ID** — the observability backbone (16.1).

### 3.2 Instrumenting the platform

`[BP]`
- **OpenTelemetry** (16.4): instrument once, export anywhere; standard across all services.
- **Golden signals per service** (14.3) + **RED** (rate/errors/duration) for services + **USE** (utilization/saturation/errors) for resources (14.3/17.1).
- **Trace the trade saga** (20.6): a distributed trace spanning compliance → reserve → execute → ledger → portfolio localizes where a trade is slow/stuck (16.4) — essential for a multi-context flow.
- **Business/financial metrics:** trade volumes, settlement times, reconciliation discrepancies (19.2.3) — not just infra.
- `[BP]` **Instrument golden signals + distributed traces (esp. the saga) + business metrics**, via OpenTelemetry.

### 3.3 SLO-based alerting + on-call (14.1 / 14.4)

`[BP]`
- **Alert on SLO burn-rate** (14.1/16.5), multi-window — not static thresholds or cause-spam; every page **urgent + actionable + impactful** (14.4 — avoid alert fatigue).
- **Symptom-based** (user-facing SLO breaches) over cause-based; route to **humane on-call** (14.4) with **runbooks**.
- **Tiered:** page (urgent) / ticket (soon) / log (info). Financial-correctness alerts (reconciliation mismatch, ledger imbalance) are **high-priority**.
- `[BP]` **SLO burn-rate alerting + humane on-call + runbooks** (14.1/14.4).

### 3.4 Incident response + postmortems (14.5)

`[BP]`
- **Structured incident response** (14.5): Incident Command (IC/Ops/Comms/Scribe); **mitigate before resolve** (stop the bleeding); observability drives fast diagnosis (low MTTR).
- **Blameless postmortems** (14.5): systems-not-people; tracked action items; human error = symptom of a system gap.
- For financial incidents: **reconciliation** (19.2.3) confirms money integrity post-incident; audit trail (20.7) reconstructs what happened.
- `[BP]` **Observability feeds structured IR + blameless postmortems** (14.5).

### 3.5 The production-readiness review (PRR)

`[CS]` The **launch gate** — is this system safe to run? A checklist synthesizing the whole course `[BP]`:
- **SLOs defined + measured** (20.2) + error budgets + burn-rate alerts (14.1).
- **Observability:** golden signals, traces, structured logs, dashboards in place (Part 16).
- **Alerting + on-call:** actionable alerts, runbooks, on-call rotation (14.4).
- **Reliability:** HA (no SPOF), **DR tested** (RPO/RTO — 11.8/20.10), capacity for peak + headroom (20.2).
- **Security + compliance:** authN/authz, encryption, secrets, audit, KYC/AML/PCI/GDPR controls (Part 15/20.11).
- **Resilience:** timeouts/retries/circuit-breakers/bulkheads on dependencies (11.3); load shedding (11.4).
- **Release safety:** CI/CD, progressive delivery (canary — 14.7), rollback, feature flags.
- **Data:** backups tested, reconciliation running (19.2.3), migrations safe (5.4.3).
- `[BP]` **The PRR is the checklist that turns "built" into "safe to operate"** — it's the operational conscience of the whole design.

### 3.6 Deep dives + bottlenecks

`[BP]`
- **Monitoring must outlive the monitored** (16.6/19.2.6): the observability stack in a **separate failure domain** — you need it most when the platform is failing.
- **Cardinality/cost** (16.2): bounded labels; high-cardinality → logs/traces, not metrics.
- **Trace sampling** (16.4): head vs tail sampling to control cost while keeping useful traces (errors/slow).
- **PII in telemetry** (15.8): redact — never log secrets/PII (16.3).
- **Bottleneck:** telemetry volume/cost → sampling, cardinality control, retention tiers (16.2); the monitoring platform itself is 19.2.6.
- `[BP]` **The lesson:** observability = **three correlated pillars (metrics detect / traces localize / logs diagnose — 16.1) via OpenTelemetry, SLO burn-rate alerting + humane on-call (14.1/14.4), structured IR + blameless postmortems (14.5)** — all gated by a **production-readiness review** that verifies SLOs, observability, reliability/DR, security/compliance, resilience, and release safety before launch. Make the observability stack **outlive the monitored** (16.6).

## 5. Real-World Analogy

Think of a **hospital's monitoring and its pre-surgery safety checklist**.

- **Three pillars = vital-sign monitors, the chart, and the diagnostic scan:** the **monitors** (metrics) beep when something's off (detect); the **scan** (traces) shows **where** in the body the problem is (localize); the **detailed chart notes** (logs) explain **what** happened (diagnose). A single **patient ID** (trace ID) ties them together so you can follow one case across all three.
- **SLO alerting = alarms tuned to matter:** the monitors don't shriek at every tiny flutter (alert fatigue) — they alarm when a vital is **trending toward danger fast** (burn-rate), and a **specialist is on call** with a **treatment protocol** (runbook).
- **Incident response = the code-blue team:** a clear **incident commander** coordinates, they **stabilize the patient first** (mitigate before resolve), and afterward hold a **blameless review** to fix the *system*, not blame a nurse.
- **Monitoring outlives the monitored = the monitors have their own power:** if the ward loses power, the vital-sign monitors keep running — that's exactly when you need them.
- **Production-readiness review = the pre-surgery checklist:** before you operate, you **verify everything** — the right patient, blood on hand (DR), sterile instruments (security), a recovery plan. Skipping the checklist is how disasters happen; running it turns "the surgeon is ready" into "it's safe to proceed."

## 8–14. (Condensed)

**Advantages:** fast detect→localize→diagnose (low MTTR); provable SLO compliance; traceable distributed flows (sagas); disciplined safe launches (PRR); auditable operations.
**Disadvantages/cautions:** telemetry cost/cardinality (control via sampling/bounded labels); PII risk in telemetry (redact); observability stack is critical infra (make it outlive the monitored); PRR is effort (worth it).
**When NOT to:** don't alert on causes/static thresholds (fatigue); don't put high-cardinality data in metrics; don't launch without a PRR for a financial system; don't share observability's fate with the platform.
**Common mistakes:** vanity dashboards; alert fatigue (non-actionable pages); no distributed tracing (can't localize); logging secrets/PII; monitoring dies with the app; skipping DR testing in the PRR.
**Interview Qs:** 🟢 What are the three pillars + their roles? 🟡 Why SLO burn-rate alerting over static thresholds? 🔴 How do you trace + debug a distributed trade saga? What's in a PRR? ⚫ Full observability + operability design + the launch gate; why must monitoring outlive the monitored?
**Production pitfalls:** blind spots (no tracing); cardinality explosion (16.2); alert fatigue; telemetry PII leaks; monitoring outage during an incident; unrehearsed incident response.
**Optimizations:** OpenTelemetry standardization; trace sampling (head/tail); bounded-cardinality metrics + logs/traces for detail; recording rules for SLOs; runbooks + automation; game days (14.8) to exercise IR.

## 15. Summary

You **cannot operate what you can't see**, and a financial platform must **detect + diagnose fast** (low MTTR — 14.5), **prove** it meets SLOs (20.2), and **audit** what happened — so **observability (Part 16)** is the operational nervous system across every context, and the **production-readiness review (PRR)** is the discipline ensuring the system is **safe to run**, not merely built. The **three pillars** are **correlated by trace ID** (16.1): **metrics** (golden signals — latency/traffic/errors/saturation — 14.3 — + per-context SLO metrics — 20.2, in a bounded-cardinality TSDB) to **detect**; **traces** (distributed across service hops incl. async — 16.4, W3C-propagated) to **localize**; and **structured, correlation-ID-stamped, secrets/PII-redacted logs** (16.3, distinct from the immutable **audit** log — 20.7) to **diagnose** — the workflow being **detect → localize → diagnose**. Instrument via **OpenTelemetry** (once, export anywhere), covering **golden signals + RED + USE** (14.3/17.1), a **distributed trace of the trade saga** (20.6 — to localize where a multi-context trade is slow/stuck), and **business/financial metrics** (trade volumes, settlement times, **reconciliation discrepancies** — 19.2.3). **Alert on SLO burn-rate** (multi-window — 14.1/16.5), not static thresholds or cause-spam — every page **urgent + actionable + impactful** (avoiding alert fatigue — 14.4) — routed to **humane on-call with runbooks**, with financial-correctness alerts (reconciliation mismatch, ledger imbalance) high-priority. Observability feeds **structured incident response** (Incident Command, **mitigate before resolve** — 14.5) and **blameless postmortems** (systems-not-people, tracked action items), with **reconciliation + the audit trail** confirming/reconstructing money integrity post-incident. The **PRR is the launch gate** — a checklist synthesizing the whole course: **SLOs defined + measured + burn-rate alerts; observability (signals/traces/logs/dashboards); actionable alerting + on-call + runbooks; reliability (no SPOF, DR tested — RPO/RTO — 20.10, capacity + headroom); security + compliance (authN/authz, encryption, secrets, audit, KYC/AML/PCI/GDPR — 20.11); resilience (breakers/bulkheads/shedding — 11.3/11.4); release safety (CI/CD, canary — 14.7, rollback, flags); data (tested backups, reconciliation, safe migrations)** — turning "built" into "safe to operate." Critically, the **observability stack must outlive the monitored** (16.6/19.2.6) — in a **separate failure domain** — because you need it most when the platform is failing; and telemetry cost is controlled via **sampling, bounded cardinality, and retention tiers** (16.2), never logging PII/secrets (15.8). In one line: **three correlated pillars via OpenTelemetry (metrics detect / traces localize / logs diagnose) + SLO burn-rate alerting + humane on-call + structured IR + blameless postmortems — gated by a production-readiness review, with the monitoring stack outliving the monitored**.
